Lebanon-Pakistan Relations

Relations between Lebanon and Pakistan are characterized by a long-standing "Treaty of Friendship" and a shared commitment to regional stability. Despite the geographical distance, both nations have maintained a steady, fraternal bond rooted in common cultural values and mutual support within international organizations like the United Nations and the Organisation of Islamic Cooperation (OIC).

Historical and Diplomatic Foundations

Diplomatic ties were formally established in the early 1950s. A cornerstone of this relationship is the Treaty of Friendship signed in Beirut on January 16, 1953, which pledged perpetual peace and closer economic and cultural cooperation.

Over the decades, the two countries have maintained resident embassies in each other's capitals (Islamabad and Beirut). High-level meetings often occur on the sidelines of global summits. For instance, in January 2024 and again in 2026, the Prime ministers of both nations met at the World Economic Forum in Davos to reaffirm their resolve to strengthen bilateral ties and discuss the regional security situation.

Economic and Technical Cooperation

While the trade volume remains modest, there has been a renewed push to formalize and expand economic ties.

  • Joint Economic Commission (JEC): In July 2022, the two countries signed an agreement to establish a JEC, which officially came into force in May 2023. This commission serves as a consultative mechanism to enhance cooperation in trade, investment, agriculture, and science and technology.
  • Trade Figures: In the 2023–2024 fiscal year, bilateral trade was approximately $12.7 million to $22 million (depending on the reporting period).
  • Pakistan's Exports: Chiefly textiles (non-knit women's suits), rice, and plastics.
  • Lebanon's Exports: Primarily blank audio media, electrical machinery, and animal fats.

Humanitarian and Defense Ties

Pakistan has frequently extended a hand of solidarity during Lebanon's times of crisis.

  • Disaster Relief: Following the 2020 Beirut port explosion, Pakistan sent 8 tonnes of relief aid. More recently, in late 2024 and early 2025, Pakistan dispatched over 100 tons of humanitarian aid—including medicines and winter clothing—to assist those affected by regional conflicts.
  • Defense & Security: There is a history of military cooperation and training exchanges. In recent years, the two nations have also signed agreements to combat illicit drug trafficking, reflecting a shared interest in internal security and the rule of law.

Current Landscape (2026)

As of early 2026, the relationship remains focused on sovereignty and stability. Pakistan consistently advocates for Lebanon’s territorial integrity on the global stage. Conversely, the Pakistani community in Lebanon, though small, serves as a vital bridge, despite recent travel advisories issued by the Pakistani Ministry of Foreign Affairs due to regional volatility.
